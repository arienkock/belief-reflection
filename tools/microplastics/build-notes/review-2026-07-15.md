_Improvement I2 from the session blueprint: an adversarial pass over the data
files (`nodes.json`, `findings.json`, `crosslinks.json`, `evidence-audit.md`)
checked against the sourcing protocol, for direction misassignment, strength
inflation, research→simple register drift (I7), claims a source does not make,

## Reviewer findings and disposition

**1. Fabricated authorship — F-ban-1 cited "Dean et al. 2023". FIXED.** The
microbead-reduction study's citation was reconstructed from search summaries
without a confirmed author or full reference. Verification against the
publisher record corrected it to **Akhbarizadeh et al. 2024, *ACS ES&T Water*
4(2):492–499** (online 2023, in issue 2024). The load-bearing numbers (up to
~86% decline in Toronto WWTP effluents, 8.4–14.3 → 2.0–2.2 particles/m³) are
confirmed against the same record. This is exactly the no-fabrication failure
the review pass exists to catch.

**2. Fabricated page numbers — F-assoc-2 cited "NEJM 390:1770–1772". FIXED.**
The NEJM correspondence identifier (NEJMc2404154, 9 May 2024) is real and
verified, but the specific page range was not confirmed and was invented.
Reworded to "New England Journal of Medicine, 9 May 2024 (letters incl.
NEJMc2404154), and authors' reply" — real identifier, no fabricated pages.

## Reviewer confirmations (considered, no change made)

- **F-cause-1 direction (SUPPORTS vs COMPLICATES).** Considered demoting the
  toxicology-harm finding to COMPLICATES by analogy to the UPF NOVA-utility
  case (where FOR evidence that addressed a *different* question was
  demoted). Retained as **SUPPORTS**: unlike that case, the toxicology
  literature addresses the *same* endpoint (harm) and some
  environmentally-relevant-dose studies do report effects — so it is a weak,
  contested FOR, not off-topic. The dose limitation is carried prominently in
  its caveat and in two AGAINST findings (F-cause-2, F-cause-3), and the
  `depends_on` splits "harm at all" from "harm at realistic dose". The leaf is
  a genuine two-sided dispute, correctly `well-evidenced`.

- **F-assoc-3 direction (COMPLICATES vs CHALLENGES).** Considered retyping the
  SAPEA/WHO "no demonstrated population-level effect" finding to CHALLENGES.
  Retained as **COMPLICATES**: these reviews predate the 2024 cardiovascular
  study and register absence-of-corroboration and non-replication rather than
  a direct null result against the specific association. The direct AGAINST on
  this leaf is the contamination critique (F-assoc-2); overstating F-assoc-3
  as counter-evidence would inflate the AGAINST side. The leaf is correctly
  **overridden to thin** (single contested FOR study; no replication).

- **No strength inflation.** Every `strong` finding is a meta-analysis,
  systematic review, or national/international expert synthesis (SAPEA;
  Foley, Bucci–Rochman meta-analyses; Koelmans risk framework). No single
  primary study is rated strong: Marfella (single cohort), Leslie (single
  study), and the VAMAS interlaboratory exercise are all `moderate`; Ragusa
  (n=6 pilot) is `thin`.

- **No banned sourcing.** No scored leaf's sole support is an advocacy or
  industry source; the audit's advocacy line correctly reports "none". Press
  coverage was used only to locate primary sources.

- **Presence/harm discipline holds (I7).** Detection findings (F-body-1,
  F-body-2) assert presence only, in both registers; the human-association
  simple register says "have measurably worse health" (associational), not
  "microplastics harm you" (causal); every controlled-harm finding carries the
  dose/concentration-realism caveat in both registers. Spot-check found no
  simple paragraph asserting more than its research counterpart.

- **Effect sizes reconcile across shared sources.** The ~17%
  environmentally-relevant-concentration figure is used consistently in the
  human-cause (F-cause-2) and ecology (F-wild-2) leaves; the VAMAS 46–129%
  reproducibility range in F-root-2 and F-rel-1; Marfella HR 4.53 (2.00–10.27)
  and the 58.4% detection once each.

- **One-sided leaf honestly flagged, not padded.** `n_measure_reliable` is
  flagged "one-sided (no FOR)" with the reason stated (the pro-reliability
  side is standardisation-in-progress, carried as COMPLICATES, not a measured
  achieved-reproducibility result). Mirrors the UPF `n_concept_reliable`
  handling.

## Residual disclosed limitations (accepted, not defects)

- `n_health_assoc` rests on a single study (Marfella 2024) plus its critique;
  no independent replication exists — flagged thin, with a standing re-search
  instruction (I8).
- `boucher-friot` supplies evidence to both `n_origin_sources` (FOR) and
  `n_policy_bans` (AGAINST); this is disclosed and the audit collapses per
  node so it cannot self-corroborate.
- The whole map is high-income-country, marine/freshwater-weighted evidence;
  external validity to other regions and to terrestrial/soil systems is a
  standing caveat.
- Two citations rest on stable article identifiers rather than full author
  lists (the human-tissue contamination systematic review, ScienceDirect
  S2666831926000081; and the toxicological-pathways review, S0304389425007095)
  — real records located in the scan, flagged here for a future full-reference
  pass.
