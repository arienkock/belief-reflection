# Independent review — Ultra-Processed Foods and Health (2026-07-15)

_Improvement I2 from the session blueprint: an independent adversarial pass
run in a **fresh context given only the sourcing protocol and the data
files** (`nodes.json`, `findings.json`, `crosslinks.json`,
`evidence-audit.md`) — NOT the author's reasoning. The reviewer checked all
scored leaves plus flagged nodes for direction misassignment, strength
inflation, research→simple register drift (I7), claims a source does not
make, and protocol compliance. Findings and their disposition below._

## Reviewer findings and disposition

**1. Direction misassignment — F-bey-3 marked COMPLICATES but argues
AGAINST. FIXED.** The reviewer noted F-bey-3 (Fang 2024: the association
"became less pronounced after diet quality was taken into account…") is a
direct argument against the leaf's claim that processing adds value beyond
nutrients, not a neutral complication. Retyped to **CHALLENGES** and added
an `empirical-contradiction` crosslink F-bey-1 ↔ F-bey-3 (survives vs.
attenuates, within the added-value leaf). Leaf remains well-evidenced
(S1/C2).

**2. Direction misassignment — F-rel-2 marked SUPPORTS but supplies no
support for the claim scored. FIXED.** The reviewer noted the pro-NOVA
defence argues *utility*, not measured *reliability* — the one thing
`n_concept_reliable` scores — so a SUPPORTS tag overstated the FOR side.
Retyped to **COMPLICATES** and reworded to say explicitly that the FOR
direction carries no independent evidence. The leaf is now honestly
one-sided (S0/C1/X1), flagged "one-sided (no FOR)" in the audit, and the
`compose-config` override reason and audit note were updated to match. This
is the more honest state: the evidence answers "can NOVA be applied
consistently?" with a well-supported *no*, and there is no genuine
independent *yes*.

**3. Register drift — F-sub-1 and the `n_assoc_subgroup` node convert
"association" to "harm/damage" in the simple register (I7). FIXED.** On a
leaf under the observational `n_assoc` dimension, the simple register said
"the harm came mostly from particular foods." Reworded F-sub-1's simple
paragraph, effect_size, and the node's simple `claim_evaluated`, `question`,
and `depends_on` to associational language ("the link to earlier death was
strongest for…", "most linked to disease"). The two remaining checker hits
(F-root-2 "test whether processing *causes* harm"; F-sub-2 "do the
*damage*") were reviewed and left: in both the research register already
uses equivalent causal/harm language, so the simple register is not
stronger.

**4. Number plausibility — F-lab-1's 95% CI (−23.8 to −23.7) is
implausibly narrow. FIXED (removed).** Although this is the interval the
source reports (a very large household-panel before-after design produces
tight intervals), a 0.1-point CE reads as a transcription artifact and adds
nothing. Dropped the parenthetical; the −23.7% point estimate (the
load-bearing figure) stays.

**5. Date inconsistency — F-gui-1 says "2025 committee / 2024 review" in
research but "in 2024" in simple. FIXED.** Reworded the simple register to
"the main US expert panel for the 2025–2030 dietary guidelines" so both
registers name the same body without a bare, ambiguous year.

## Reviewer confirmations (no change needed)

- **No strength inflation.** Every `strong` finding is a review/synthesis or
  national-body report; no single primary study is rated strong; no
  meta-analysis is rated thin. Several systematic reviews are conservatively
  rated moderate.
- **No banned sourcing.** No scored leaf's sole support is an advocacy/
  think-tank source. The 2025 Lancet UPF Series (NOVA proponents) is carried
  as labelled position evidence bundled with the independent Nordic review,
  never as sole support (F-gui-2). The one proponent-only finding (F-rel-2)
  is now COMPLICATES, not counted as FOR evidence.
- **Contested evidence carries its dispute.** The pivotal `n_assoc_confound`
  leaf retains both the survives-adjustment (F-con-1) and attenuates
  (F-con-2) findings as a typed empirical-contradiction, with over/under-
  control caveats on both.
- **One-sided leaves honestly flagged, not padded.** `n_assoc_subgroup` and
  `n_policy_labels` (and now `n_concept_reliable`) are flagged in the audit
  with the reason stated; missing directions are declared, not fabricated.
- **Effect sizes reconcile across shared sources** (F-bey-1↔F-con-1 64/66 &
  136/142; F-cau-3↔F-root-2 30/42; F-cau-1 +500 kcal/+0.9 kg).

## Residual disclosed limitations (accepted, not defects)

- `n_assoc_magnitude` counts two FOR clusters (lane-umbrella, nordic-scoping)

- `n_assoc_subgroup` concentration rests on a single cohort; no independent
  subgroup cohort was located under the protocol — flagged thin.
- The whole map is high-income-country evidence; external validity is a
  standing caveat.
